Spinach Tofu Scramble

Author: Maja Vujic

Description

Recipe modified from: simplyrecipes.com

Tofu is pretty neutral in flavour, and easily absorbs flavours of other ingredients, making it a dynamic and versatile ingredient to add. This recipe requires firm or extra firm tofu which holds its shape better than silken tofu. For best flavour absorption in the tofu, press the tofu under two flat plates with some weight or apply light pressure to squeeze the water out. This ensures better absorption of the surrounding ingredients' flavor profile.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup shallots, diced (can sub red/yellow/white onions)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 pound extra firm tofu, drained and pressed (can sub firm tofu)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (adjust for your palette)
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper (can sub crushed red chillies)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground turmeric (can sub cumin/coriander/paprika powder)
  • 4 cups fresh spinach leaves (can also sub kale)
  • 1/4 teaspoon lemon juice (can sub 1/8 teaspoon white vinegar)

Steps

  1. Cook onions and ginger-garlic paste

    2. Heat a skillet on medium heat and add oil. When it ripples, add and sautee the onions and ginger garlic paste together until raw smell disappears.

  2. Add tofu and seasonings

    3. Crumble the tofu into bite sized pieces onto the skillet. Add salt, pepper and optional seasonings. Cook for 3 minutes, stirring every few seconds.

  3. Add spinach or kale

    4. Add the leaves and cook until dark and wilted (no more than 3 minutes). Taste, and add seasonings if necessary. Add lemon juice or white vinegar and remove from heat immediately.

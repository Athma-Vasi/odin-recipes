Easy Vegetarian Chilli

Author: Frank Holleman

Description

Recipe modified from: simplyrecipes.com

Vegetarian chilli is a quick and easy meal that is astoundingly versatile. Requiring nothing more than some imagination, it lets you mix and match ingredients, making it a great recipe to use those back of the pantry items that lie almost forgotten.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 8 ounces baby portabella mushrooms, cleaned and quartered
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 3/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 onion (red/ yellow/ white)
  • 2 shallots
  • 1 cup green bell pepper chopped (can sub yellow/ red)
  • 1 jalapeno pepper (can sub serrano, or for extra kick: thai chillies)
  • 2 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon paprika (can sub cayenne or mild kashmiri chilli powder)
  • 1/4 teaspoon coriander (can sub powder)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin (can sub powder)
  • 1/8 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/8 teaspoon cloves
  • 1/8 teaspoon cardammom
  • 1/8 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar (can sub white/ golden)
  • 1 (15 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup bulghur wheat
  • 2 (15 ounce) cans kidney beans(can sub chickpeas/ pinto)
  • 1/4 cup cilantro chopped
  • 1/4 cup green onions chopped
  • 1 avocado diced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (can sub 1/2 tablespoon white vinegar)

Steps

  1. Prep and begin cooking mushrooms:

    2. Pulse the mushrooms in a food procesor with a bladed attachment for 30 seconds. Heat oil in a large dutch oven or skillet with tall sides. Add mushrooms when oil starts rippling. Add half the salt and cook until mushrooms release liquid and turn dark (about 7 mins)

  2. Sautee veggies and peppers:

    3. Add onion to food processor (no need to clean) and pulse until onions are about pea size. Add the onions, chopped bell peppers, jalapenos, ginger-garlic paste and sautee until raw ginger-garlic smell disappears (1 - 2 mins)

  3. Add tomatoes and dry spices and simmer:

    4. Add the tomatoes and their juices. Add all the dry spices onto the wet tomatoes. Simmer for few minutes until reduced by half. Add 3 cups of water. Cover pot and bring to boil. Add the bulghur wheat, reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes. Add beans, bring to boil, reduce heat and simmer for about 7 minutes (adjust cooking time based on your preference for bulghur wheat texture).

  4. Season and serve:

    5. Add remaining salt (adjust to your palette). Remove from heat and add lemon juice (or vinegar). Garnish with chopped cilantro, green onions and diced avocados.

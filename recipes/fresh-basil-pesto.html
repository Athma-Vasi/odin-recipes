Fresh Basil Pasta

Image: Artur Rutkowski

Description

Recipe modified from: simplyrecipes.com

Pesto is a sauce originating in Genoa, the capital city of Liguria, Italy. Dating back to the Roman times, pesto traces its name to the Italian word pestare, meaning to crush or pound as the sauce was made by pounding the ingredients in a mortar and pestle.

This is a modified version with coconut cream instead of hard cheeses.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves (can substitute half with thai basil for extra punch)
  • 1/4 cup coconut cream (freshly squeezed or canned)
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil (can substitute coconut oil or avocado oil)
  • 1/3 cup pine nuts (can substitute walnuts or sunflower seeds)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced (about 1 tablespoon)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt (adjust to your palette)
  • 1/8 teaspoon pepper (adjust to your palette)
  • (optional) 1/4 teaspoon ginger
  • (optional) 1/8 teaspoon crushed red chillies

Steps

  1. Pulse the basil and nuts:

    2. Place basil leaves and pine nuts into a food processor and pulse several times.

  2. Add the garlic and coconut cream(optional: ginger):

    3. Add the garlic and coconut cream and pulse several times. If you wish it to be more silky, pulse until well mixed. Otherwise, pulse once every second and then check.

  3. Slowly pour in the olive oil (or other oils):

    4. Slowly add in the oil in small amounts, then pulse few times, scrape the sides, and repeat.

  4. Season the pesto sauce:

    5. Add salt, black pepper (optional: crushed red chillies).

    Toss with pasta, dollop over baked potatoes, or spread onto crackers or toasted bread.
